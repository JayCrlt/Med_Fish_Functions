Figure 1 | Global distribution and compositional structure of marine fish assemblages.
(A) Location of the 38 marine ecoregions included in the analysis. Hexagons indicate survey locations, colored according to the duration of the time series (number of years sampled). Numbers identify ecoregions. (B) Non-metric multidimensional scaling (NMDS) ordination of fish assemblages based on species biomass. Points represent annual assemblages across all ecoregions. Shaded polygons indicate the 99%, 75%, and 50% highest-density regions (HDRs) of the ordination space. Fish silhouettes illustrate characteristic taxa associated with different regions of the assemblage space. NMDS stress = 0.17. (C–H) Temporal trajectories of annual fish assemblages in NMDS space for six major geographic regions: Eastern Pacific, Western Atlantic, Eastern Atlantic, North and Baltic Seas, Barents Sea, and Mediterranean Sea. Points represent annual assemblage states and are colored by year. Black contours show the global 99%, 75%, and 50% HDRs from panel b. For each region, n denotes the number of ecoregions included and the value in km² indicates the total surveyed area.

Figure 3 | Recent changes in ecosystem functioning relative to the historical baseline across 38 marine ecoregions. For each ecoregion, historical observations are shown as grey points representing the distribution of annual cell-level estimates for carbon production (A), nitrogen excretion (B), phosphorus excretion (C), benthivory (D), piscivory (E), planktivory (F), and the multifunctionality index (G). Fluxes were standardized independently within each ecoregion relative to their historical mean and standard deviation, such that 0 represents the historical average and values are expressed as standard deviation units (σ). Colored circles indicate the median anomaly across the five most recent years available for each ecoregion, and horizontal bars denote the interquartile range (25th–75th percentiles). Filled circles identify ecoregions whose recent interquartile range lies entirely above or below the historical mean, whereas open circles indicate overlap with the historical baseline. Dashed vertical lines denote the historical mean (0 σ), and dotted horizontal lines separate the major ocean basins.

Fig. S2 | Monthly baseline sea bottom temperature at sampled grid cells. 
Grid cells indicate locations where at least one survey was conducted. Cell colors show the climatological mean sea bottom temperature (°C) for each calendar month, calculated separately for each grid cell from the Copernicus Marine Service global physical reanalysis (cmems_mod_glo_phy_my_0.083deg_P1M-m) by averaging all monthly bottom temperature estimates for the corresponding month over 1993–2025.
